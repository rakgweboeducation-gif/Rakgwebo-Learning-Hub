The Chat & Whiteboard functionality is the collaborative help space inside your app. It combines normal messaging with a live drawing board so learners can explain maths visually, not just with text.

Here’s what it does in simple terms:

1) Normal chat (like WhatsApp or Messenger)

Inside the Chat module, users can:

Send text messages

Send emojis

Attach images or documents

Send voice notes

See messages in real time

So it works like a regular messaging app between:

Learner ↔ Learner

Learner ↔ Tutor

Learner ↔ Teacher

2) Interactive whiteboard inside the chat

Each conversation can open a shared whiteboard where users can:

Draw equations

Sketch graphs

Show geometric constructions

Highlight steps in a solution

Erase and redo work

This is especially useful for maths because:

Many concepts need diagrams

It’s easier to explain visually than in text

3) Whiteboard recording (1-minute explanation)

On the whiteboard, a user can:

Click Record

Solve or explain something on the board

Stop recording (max: 60 seconds)

The app will:

Turn that into a short video

Send it directly into the chat as a message

So it becomes like:

“Here’s how to solve this question”
(with a short visual explanation)

4) How it connects to the textbook

When a learner highlights or writes on a textbook:

They click Ask for Help

A chat opens with another user

The recipient sees:

The exact page

The highlighted/drawn part

They can reply using:

Text

Voice

Whiteboard explanation

Real example scenario

Grade 11 learner is stuck on a trigonometry question.

They highlight the question in the textbook.

Click Ask for Help.

Send it to a friend or tutor.

Tutor opens chat and:

Opens whiteboard

Draws triangle

Records a 45-second explanation

Learner watches the explanation and understands.

In short

Chat = conversation
Whiteboard = visual explanation space
Recording = short video solution

Together, they create a maths-focused communication system, not just a normal messaging app.
